GEORGE WASHINGTON CARVER

1864 – January 5, 1943

George Washington Carver was an American agricultural, botanist, scientist, inventor (who promoted alternative crops to cotton and methods to prevent soil depletion) and Professor at Tuskegee Institute.
Much of his research and subsequent contributions to agriculture involved new techniques for improving and sustaining soil quality.
The societal and philosophical motivation for his scientific work was to provide farmers the tools and techniques to self-sustain a high quality of life through the success of their crops.
He was the most prominent black scientist of the early 20th century

Timeline of George Washington Carver

  1. 1860 – George Washington Carver is born under slavery in Diamond, Missouri.
  2. 1890 – First black student at Simpson College, studying art and piano. His art teacher, Etta Budd, recognized Carver's talent for painting flowers and plants and encouraged him to study botany at Iowa State Agricultural College in Ames.
  3. 1891 – Enrolls at Iowa State Agricultural College.
  4. 1894 – Earns bachelor's degree at Iowa State Agricultural College and upon encouragement from Iowa State professors Joseph Budd and Louis Pammel, begins enters the Master's program. Becomes first black faculty member of Iowa State.
  5. 1896 – Graduates from Iowa State. Accepts faculty position at Tuskegee Institute in Tuskegee Alabama.
  6. 1896 – Carver designs a mobile teaching facility dubbed the Jesup wagon to educate southern farmers. It was funded by New York financier and philanthropist Morris Ketchum Jesup.
  7. 1915 – Carver begins to experiment and conduct research on new uses of peanuts, soybeans, pecans and sweet potatoes.
  8. 1916 – Publishes the agricultural bulletin, How to Grow the Peanut and 105 Ways of Preparing it for Human Consumption
  9. 1916 – Inducted into the Royal Society of Arts in England.
  10. 1920 – Gives a speech at the Peanut Growers Association on the different ways in which peanuts can be used. Speaks at the United Peanut Associations of America, elaborating on 'The Possibilities of the Peanut' and demonstrated 145 peanut products
  11. 1921 – Gives testimony before the U.S. Congress for a tariff on imported peanuts to protect U.S. peanut farmers.
  12. 1923 – Received the Spingarn Medal, conferred by NAACP annually for outstanding achievement.
  13. 1928 – he received an honorary doctorate from Simpson College.
  14. 1939 – he received the Roosevelt Medal for 'Outstanding Contribution to Southern Agriculture'.
  15. 1943 – Died in hospital after falling down stairs in his home.
